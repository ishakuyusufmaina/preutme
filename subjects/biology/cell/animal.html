An animal cell is the basic structural and functional unit of animals. It is eukaryotic, meaning it has a true nucleus enclosed by a membrane and various specialized organelles that perform specific tasks. Animal cells are typically round or irregular in shape and are surrounded by a flexible cell membrane that controls the movement of substances in and out of the cell.

Key Parts of an Animal Cell:

  • Nucleus: Contains genetic material (DNA) and controls cell activities.
  • Cytoplasm: A jelly-like substance where chemical reactions occur and organelles are suspended.
  • Mitochondria: Known as the "powerhouse" of the cell, they produce energy through cellular respiration.
  • Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER): Involved in protein and lipid synthesis.
  • Golgi Apparatus: Packages and transports proteins and other materials.
  • Lysosomes: Contain digestive enzymes to break down waste and cellular debris.

Animal cells differ from plant cells because they lack a cell wall and chloroplasts but are highly specialized to perform various functions in multicellular organisms.
